THE MATHEMATICS OF SLOTS

Expected Value (EV)
The fundamental formula: EV = Σ(P(outcome) × payout). For the house to profit, EV must be negative for the player. Every spin, the casino extracts the house edge.

Return to Player (RTP)
RTP is the long-run percentage of all bets returned to players. A 94% RTP means for every BND 100 wagered, players receive BND 94 back on average — the house keeps BND 6 (6% house edge).

Law of Large Numbers
Individual spins are volatile — a player can win big in a single session. But over thousands of spins, actual RTP converges to the theoretical RTP. The house edge is mathematically guaranteed over time.

Variance & Volatility
High-variance slots (Ups & Downs mode) have bigger swings but the same house edge. Low-variance slots (Small Wins mode) give frequent small wins. Both yield the same long-run profit — variance only affects the shape of the loss curve.

The Gambler's Fallacy
Each spin is statistically independent. A losing streak does not increase the probability of the next win. Jackpot Time mode demonstrates this: even with 2 forced jackpots in 100 spins, the remaining losses ensure the house maintains its edge.
